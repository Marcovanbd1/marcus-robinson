The problem

The consultation ends.
What does the patient

01 — The gap
After the appointment
A patient leaves a GP consultation. Or a hospital discharge. Or calls 111. In each case, there's a clinical record — but what navigates the patient forward? Mostly: a leaflet, if they're lucky. A vague suggestion to "look online." Nothing curated to their actual situation.
The navigation gap is where health outcomes diverge
02 — The existing tools
Documentation is not navigation
AI in healthcare is moving fast — Dragon Copilot, Magic Notes, Heidi, TORTUS. All of them are solving the same problem: reducing clinician admin burden, getting better documentation. None of them answer the question: what does the service user receive, in their hands, to navigate forward?
Upstream solved. Downstream still empty.
03 — The opportunity
Platform-agnostic intelligence
Support Playlists doesn't require any particular consultation tool. It can sit downstream of any input — spoken or typed. It's not a product fighting for the clinical record. It's a layer that turns whatever already exists into something the patient can use.
Works with what's already there.
The concept

What Support Playlists actually does

Speak or type. LLM sense-making. Curated next steps — NHS resources and local services, in order. Not a chatbot. Not a search. Navigation intelligence.

What it is not
Not diagnosis. Not documentation. Not a chatbot.
Support Playlists sits downstream of the clinical encounter. It doesn't loop back into clinical decision-making. That's a regulatory and governance feature, not a limitation — it means Class I medical device territory. No MHRA certification burden. Deployable fast.
What it produces
A personalised, sequenced navigation resource
A patient who just received a diabetes diagnosis gets a Playlist: NHS resources, local support groups, pharmacy services, community activities, self-management tools — in an order that makes sense for where they are right now.
  • GP post-consultation handoff
  • Hospital discharge navigation
  • Community self-referral
  • AVT workflow output — the last step that's currently missing
  • Proactive, at any point in a patient journey
